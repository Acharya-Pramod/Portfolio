About

Dr. Acharya Pramod has earned his PhD in experimental particle physics, specializing in low mass dark matter search utilizing low energy threshold high purity germanium detector. His research encompasses generating and simulating cosmic-ray muon events, energy calibration using various particles, and detailed analysis of particle reconstruction and identification within the DUNE FD. Additionally, he has made significant contributions to hardware and instrumentation work, particularly in the area of Liquid Argon Time Projection Chamber (LArTPC) R&D, demonstrating a holistic approach to advancing particle physics.

Outside academia, Dr. Acharya finds solace in music, literature, and sports, particularly football and cricket. With a diverse skill set and a solid foundation in both analytical and hardware aspects of particle physics, he stands motivated to make substantial contributions to the field, shaping the future landscape of high-energy physics research.
